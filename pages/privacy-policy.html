How Binary Findings collects, uses and protects your personal information.

# Privacy Policy **Last updated:** 24 July 2026

1. Introduction

Binary Findings™ ("we", "our", or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy and handling your personal information responsibly. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store and protect your personal data when you visit our website or contact us. By using this website, you acknowledge that you have read this Privacy Policy.

2. Who We Are

Binary Findings™ provides specialist data consultancy and analytical services across healthcare and other sectors. **Website:** https://www.binaryfindings.co.uk **Email:** [enquiries@binaryfindings.co.uk](mailto:enquiries@binaryfindings.co.uk)

3. Information We Collect

We may collect personal information that you voluntarily provide, including: * Your name * Organisation * Job title * Email address * Telephone number * Information you provide through enquiry forms * Information submitted when requesting a quotation * Information provided when booking a consultation * Any information included in emails or correspondence with us We do not intentionally collect special category personal data through our website. Please avoid including sensitive personal information unless specifically requested.

4. How We Use Your Information

We use your information to: * Respond to enquiries. * Prepare quotations. * Arrange consultations. * Communicate regarding our services. * Deliver services you have requested. * Meet legal and regulatory obligations. * Maintain records of our business communications. We do not sell your personal information.

5. Lawful Basis for Processing

Where UK data protection law applies, we process personal information on one or more of the following legal bases: * Your consent. * Taking steps prior to entering into a contract. * Performance of a contract. * Compliance with legal obligations. * Our legitimate interests in operating and improving our business.

6. Information Security

We take appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal information from unauthorised access, loss, misuse or disclosure. Where appropriate, information is stored using secure Microsoft 365 services and other reputable business systems with appropriate security controls.

7. Sharing Your Information

We do not sell or rent your personal information. Your information may be shared with trusted service providers where necessary to operate our business, including: * Microsoft 365 services (such as Outlook, Microsoft Forms and Microsoft Bookings) * Website hosting providers * Professional advisers where legally required We will only share information where appropriate and in accordance with applicable data protection legislation.

8. Data Retention

We retain personal information only for as long as reasonably necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes and enforce our agreements. When information is no longer required, it will be securely deleted or anonymised where appropriate.

9. Your Rights

Under UK data protection legislation, you may have the right to: * Request access to your personal information. * Request correction of inaccurate information. * Request deletion of your personal information. * Request restriction of processing. * Object to certain processing activities. * Request transfer of your information where applicable. * Withdraw consent where processing is based on consent. To exercise these rights, please contact us using the details below.

10. Cookies

Our website may use essential cookies required for the operation of the website. If additional cookies, such as analytics cookies, are introduced in the future, this Privacy Policy and our Cookie Policy will be updated accordingly.

11. Third-Party Websites

Our website may contain links to external websites. We are not responsible for the privacy practices or content of those websites. We encourage you to read their privacy policies before providing personal information.

12. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our services, legal obligations or business practices. The latest version will always be available on this website.

13. Contact Us

If you have any questions regarding this Privacy Policy or how your personal information is handled, please contact: **Binary Findings™** Email: [enquiries@binaryfindings.co.uk](mailto:enquiries@binaryfindings.co.uk) Website: https://www.binaryfindings.co.uk

14. Complaints

If you are unhappy with how we have handled your personal information, we would welcome the opportunity to address your concerns. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO): https://ico.org.uk/
